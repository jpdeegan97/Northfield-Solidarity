1. Purpose

This SOP governs the creation, operation, and control of brands, storefronts, digital properties, pricing, promotions, and customer-facing representations across the Northfield Solidarity ecosystem.

This SOP exists to:

  • Protect and consistently express Northfield Solidarity brands
  • Ensure storefronts operate within approved economic and legal boundaries
  • Prevent unauthorized brand usage, pricing erosion, or reputational risk
  • Align brand execution with Revenue, Fulfillment, IP, Compliance, and Parent governance

Risks mitigated:

  • Brand dilution or inconsistent messaging
  • Margin destruction through unapproved pricing or promotions
  • Regulatory exposure (advertising, consumer protection)
  • Unauthorized use of IP or trademarks

Outcome ensured:

  • Cohesive brand governance across all channels
  • Predictable, compliant store operations
  • Clear authority and escalation for brand-impacting decisions

2. Scope

Entities Covered:

  • Northfield Solidarity (Parent / HoldCo)
  • Brand-holding entities
  • E-commerce, digital, and physical storefront operating entities

Roles Covered:

  • Brand Lead
  • Store Operations Manager
  • Marketing Manager
  • Finance Lead
  • Compliance Officer
  • Authorized Signatories

Activities Included:

  • Brand creation and positioning
  • Storefront setup and operation
  • Pricing and promotion management
  • Marketing content and advertising
  • Customer experience standards
  • Brand performance monitoring
  • Exception handling and escalation

Explicit Exclusions:

  • Revenue recognition and billing (Revenue Flow SOP v1.2)
  • Fulfillment execution (Fulfillment & Logistics SOP v1.2)
  • IP ownership and licensing mechanics (IP & Licensing SOP v1.2)

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Brand integrity supersedes short-term revenue optimization
  • Pricing must remain above approved economic floors
  • All brand usage must align with approved IP rights
  • Customer-facing claims must be accurate and compliant
  • Material brand deviations trigger Compliance SOP v1.2
  • Parent / HoldCo retains final authority over brand strategy

6. Process Overview

Inputs:

  • Approved brand standards
  • Product catalog and pricing models
  • Marketing campaigns and content

Key Steps:

  1. Brand and store setup
  2. Pricing and promotion configuration
  3. Marketing content deployment
  4. Live storefront operation
  5. Performance monitoring
  6. Exception detection
  7. Compliance escalation
  8. Documentation storage

Outputs:

  • Live storefronts
  • Approved pricing and promotions
  • Brand performance reports
  • Compliance Event Logs (if applicable)

7. Detailed Procedures

7.1 Brand & Store Setup

  • Action: Establish storefronts using approved brand assets and standards
  • Owner: Brand Lead / Store Operations Manager
  • Output: Approved, live storefront

7.2 Pricing Configuration

  • Action: Set pricing in accordance with approved pricing floors
  • Owner: Finance Lead with Brand Lead review
  • Output: Approved pricing matrix

7.3 Promotions & Discounts

  • Action: Implement promotions within approved thresholds
  • Owner: Marketing Manager
  • Output: Active promotion
  • Compliance: Escalation required if pricing floor is breached

7.4 Marketing Content Deployment

  • Action: Publish marketing and advertising content
  • Owner: Marketing Manager
  • Output: Live campaigns
  • Compliance: Content reviewed for regulatory and claim accuracy

7.5 Storefront Operations

  • Action: Operate storefront, monitor UX, and manage updates
  • Owner: Store Operations Manager
  • Output: Ongoing storefront performance

7.6 Performance Monitoring

  • Action: Track conversion, margin, and customer metrics
  • Owner: Brand Lead / Finance Lead
  • Output: Performance dashboards

7.7 Compliance Escalation

  • Action: Escalate brand exceptions, regulatory risks, or reputational issues
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Compliance Event Log and Parent notification if material

7.8 Documentation Storage

  • Action: Store brand assets, pricing approvals, campaigns, and exceptions
  • Owner: Brand Lead
  • Output: Audit-ready brand records

8. Controls & Compliance

  • Pricing floors enforced in systems
  • Segregation of duties between brand, marketing, and finance
  • Mandatory review of high-impact campaigns
  • Compliance SOP v1.2 governs all brand exceptions
  • Parent approval for material brand changes

9. Records & Documentation

10. KPIs & Monitoring

  • Conversion rate

11. Exceptions & Escalation

  • Pricing below floor: Immediate Compliance escalation
  • Unauthorized brand use: Brand Lead → Compliance → Parent
  • Regulatory complaints: Compliance Officer → Parent
  • Emergency brand actions logged post-event
