1. Purpose

This SOP defines a reinforced and executable framework for identifying, responding to, and recovering from material risks and disruption events across the Northfield Solidarity group.

It exists to:


  • Preserve operational continuity under stress
  • Protect liquidity, access, and reputation
  • Establish decisive authority during incidents
  • Provide documented, defensible actions during adverse events

Outcome ensured:

  • The organization remains operable, solvent, and compliant during disruptions

2. Scope

Entities Covered

  • Parent / HoldCo
  • All operating, digital commerce, and service subsidiaries

Risk Domains Covered

  • Banking and payment processor freezes or terminations
  • Platform bans, suspensions, or shadow restrictions
  • Regulatory, legal, or compliance enforcement actions
  • Vendor, logistics, or fulfillment collapse
  • Data loss, cyber events, or system outages
  • Key-person or capability dependency

Explicit Exclusions

  • Strategic market risk (covered by strategy planning)
  • Routine operational issues governed by functional SOPs

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Continuity over growth
  • Cash and access preservation first
  • Single-threaded command authority
  • Pre-approved playbooks reduce decision latency
  • Temporary measures must be documented
  • Normal governance resumes post-incident

8. Scenario Playbooks (Expanded)

8.1 Bank Account Freeze

  • Freeze non-essential payments
  • Activate secondary banking relationships
  • Shift receivables where possible
  • Engage counsel if regulatory
  • Log all communications

8.2 Payment Processor Termination

  • Reroute traffic to backup processor
  • Pause paid spend immediately
  • Communicate internally within 24 hours

8.3 Platform Suspension / Ban

  • Assess scope (account vs entity vs brand)
  • Activate alternate storefronts or channels
  • Preserve customer, order, and analytics data
  • Prepare appeal or migration strategy

8.4 Fulfillment or Vendor Collapse

  • Reassign orders to secondary vendors
  • Adjust customer delivery expectations
  • Prioritize high-value or regulated orders

8.5 Data Breach or System Failure

  • Isolate affected systems
  • Activate backups
  • Restrict changes during stabilization
  • Notify Compliance immediately

8.6 Key-Person Loss

  • Activate delegation matrix
  • Transfer credentials and access
  • Pause non-critical initiatives

9. Financial & Compliance Controls

  • Emergency spending caps defined by severity
  • Parent approval required for:
  • New accounts
  • Emergency contracts
  • Material customer communications
  • Post-incident reconciliation mandatory

10. Communication Protocols
