NSDC Engine Charter — CDE (Content Distribution Engine)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Content Distribution Engine

Acronym: CDE

Engine Type: Distribution / Publishing / Engagement

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to manage, orchestrate, and distribute content across multiple platforms and channels, ensuring consistent messaging, optimized engagement, and alignment with NSDC strategy.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Orchestrate and publish content across multiple channels (social, marketplaces, newsletters, etc.)
  • Schedule and optimize distribution based on engagement metrics
  • Track performance, reach, and engagement of content
  • Integrate with intelligence engines (PIE, SIG) for content targeting
  • Maintain historical content archives and distribution logs
  • Provide feedback to planning and governance layers

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Execute trades or arbitrage opportunities (DAT)
  • Govern capital or compliance policies (GGP)
  • Generate canonical product intelligence (PIE)
  • Maintain financial ledgers (FLO)
  • Integrate marketplaces beyond content deployment (MUX)
  • Simulate scenarios or forecast financial performance (SIM)

CDE is strictly responsible for content distribution and engagement tracking.

5. Inputs (What This Engine Consumes)

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

CDE May Decide Autonomously:

  • Scheduling and sequencing of content distribution
  • Optimizing delivery timing based on engagement metrics
  • Selecting platform-specific formatting or presentation

CDE Must Not Decide:

  • Capital deployment or execution
  • Product intelligence or arbitrage strategy
  • Governance policy enforcement beyond content compliance
  • Simulation or forecasting beyond content metrics

CDE is a distribution and engagement engine only.

8. Governance & Constraints

  • All content distribution must comply with GGP rules
  • Approval workflows must be enforced for sensitive content
  • Logs must be maintained for all published actions
  • Engagement and performance tracking must be accurate and verifiable

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Content delivery failures or delays
  • Misformatted or incomplete content
  • Tracking and engagement reporting inconsistencies

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Missed campaigns, low engagement, or incorrect content delivery
  • Minor reputational impact if errors are external-facing

Blast Radius:

  • Primarily impacts audience engagement and downstream intelligence (PIE, SIG)
  • No direct financial or capital impact

Containment strategy:

  • Retry and fallback distribution mechanisms
  • Logging and alerting of failures
  • Versioned content archives for restoration

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Engagement, reach, and content effectiveness
  • Time Horizon: Real-time for immediate campaigns, near real-time for scheduled distribution

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Content assets and media files
  • Audience and targeting signals (SIG / PIE)
  • Distribution channels and APIs (MUX)
  • Governance flags and approval workflows (GGP)
  • Engagement metrics and reporting schema

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Content orchestration and publishing across 2–3 primary channels
  • Engagement tracking and reporting
  • Historical content archives
  • Integration with PIE and SIG for targeting signals

MVP Excludes:

  • Predictive content optimization beyond initial metrics
  • Execution of financial or product strategy
  • Integration with all marketplaces beyond content channels

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Core content publishing and engagement tracking
  • Phase 2: Multi-channel optimization and audience targeting
  • Phase 3: Predictive content optimization and automated campaign orchestration
