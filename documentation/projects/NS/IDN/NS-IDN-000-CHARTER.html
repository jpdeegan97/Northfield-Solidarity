NSDC Engine Charter — IDN (Identity & Entity Nexus)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Identity & Entity Nexus

Acronym: IDN

Engine Type: Identity / Entity Management / Canonical Reference

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to maintain canonical identity, entity, and relationship records across NSDC, providing a single source of truth for all actors, assets, and organizational structures.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Maintain canonical records of entities, users, accounts, and organizational structures
  • Track relationships and ownership hierarchies between entities and assets
  • Provide unique identifiers and references for NSDC engines
  • Serve as a reference point for governance, execution, and analytics engines
  • Maintain historical snapshots of entity state for auditability
  • Ensure consistency of identity data across all NSDC systems

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Execute trades or arbitrage (DAT)
  • Govern capital or compliance (GGP)
  • Generate product or market intelligence (PIE)
  • Publish content or manage engagement (CDE)
  • Maintain financial ledgers (FLO)

IDN provides canonical identity; it does not perform operations.

5. Inputs (What This Engine Consumes)

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

IDN May Decide Autonomously:

  • Assign and maintain unique identifiers
  • Track and enforce entity hierarchies
  • Validate consistency of identity data across engines

IDN Must Not Decide:

  • Execution of trades or capital deployment
  • Governance or policy enforcement outside reference
  • Product, content, or market strategy decisions

IDN is the canonical reference system, not a decision-making engine.

8. Governance & Constraints

  • All entity modifications must comply with GGP rules
  • Historical state must be versioned and immutable
  • Referential integrity must be maintained across NSDC engines
  • Access controls and permissions must be enforced for entity modifications

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Duplicate or conflicting identifiers
  • Relationship hierarchy inconsistencies
  • Delays in entity propagation

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Downstream engines receive inconsistent identity data, leading to operational errors
  • Potential audit or compliance issues

Blast Radius:

  • Primarily impacts governance (GGP), execution (DAT), and financial tracking (FLO)
  • Does not directly cause financial loss

Containment strategy:

  • Validation and reconciliation of entity IDs
  • Versioned snapshots and rollback
  • Alerts for inconsistencies

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Accuracy, uniqueness, and consistency of identity data
  • Time Horizon: Continuous real-time reference with historical state available for audits

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Unique entity IDs
  • Canonical asset and account references
  • Relationship and hierarchy schemas
  • Historical snapshot records
  • Governance flags and permissions (GGP)

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Creation and maintenance of unique entity identifiers
  • Tracking entity relationships and ownership hierarchies
  • Historical snapshots for auditing
  • Real-time reference API for NSDC engines

MVP Excludes:

  • Execution, capital deployment, or trading
  • Product or market intelligence generation
  • Content distribution

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Core entity creation, hierarchy mapping, and reference API
  • Phase 2: Integration with all NSDC engines and audit-ready versioning
  • Phase 3: Advanced relationship modeling and dynamic entity analytics
