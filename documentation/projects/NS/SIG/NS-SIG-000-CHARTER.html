NSDC Engine Charter — SIG (Signal Aggregation Engine)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Signal Aggregation Engine

Acronym: SIG

Engine Type: Intelligence / Signal Processing

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to collect, aggregate, and process multiple external and internal signals—including market trends, social, sentiment, and alternative data sources—to provide actionable signals for NSDC decision-making.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Aggregate signals from diverse sources: marketplaces, news, social media, APIs, and internal engines
  • Normalize and structure signals for consumption
  • Filter, rank, and prioritize signals based on relevance and reliability
  • Feed actionable signals to execution engines (DAT) and intelligence engines (PIE)
  • Maintain historical signal archives for backtesting and analysis
  • Provide alerts for significant or emergent events

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Directly execute trades or arbitrage (DAT)
  • Govern capital or risk policies (GGP)
  • Create product intelligence or canonical product records (PIE)
  • Distribute content or manage engagement (CDE)
  • Maintain financial ledgers (FLO)

SIG is purely a signal collection and processing engine.

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

SIG May Decide Autonomously:

  • How to normalize, rank, and prioritize signals
  • Signal relevance scoring and filtering
  • Alert thresholds for emergent events

SIG Must Not Decide:

  • When to execute trades or deploy capital
  • Governance policy enforcement
  • Product development or content strategies

SIG informs decision-making; it does not act directly.

8. Governance & Constraints

  • Signal collection and processing must comply with GGP policies
  • Maintain audit-ready logs for all signal ingestion and processing
  • Data integrity and accuracy checks must be enforced
  • Alerts must adhere to governance-defined escalation paths

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Signal feed interruption or errors
  • Misclassification or misranking of signals
  • Latency in processing

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Downstream engines act on incorrect or incomplete signals
  • Missed opportunities due to delayed signals

Blast Radius:

  • Primarily impacts DAT execution and PIE insights
  • No direct financial or compliance violation

Containment strategy:

  • Signal validation and redundancy
  • Alerting on anomalies
  • Historical signal backup for recovery

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Signal accuracy, relevance, and timeliness
  • Time Horizon: Real-time for emergent events, near real-time for general trends

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Canonical entity IDs (IDN)
  • Product IDs and attributes (PIE)
  • Market identifiers and API endpoints (MUX)
  • Execution references (DAT)
  • Governance flags (GGP)

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Aggregation from 2–3 primary signal sources
  • Normalization and relevance scoring
  • Alerts for emergent events
  • Logging and historical archiving

MVP Excludes:

  • Predictive modeling for signals beyond relevance scoring
  • Integration with all possible signal sources beyond MVP
  • Execution of trades or direct actions

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Signal aggregation, normalization, and ranking
  • Phase 2: Multi-source integration with advanced prioritization and scoring
  • Phase 3: Predictive signal modeling and automated recommendation for execution engines
