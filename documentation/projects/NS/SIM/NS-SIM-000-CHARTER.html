NSDC Engine Charter — SIM (Simulation & Scenario Engine)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Simulation & Scenario Engine

Acronym: SIM

Engine Type: Modeling / Forecasting / Analysis

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to simulate, model, and analyze potential scenarios and system behaviors within NSDC, enabling data-driven decision-making and strategic forecasting across engines and projects.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Build models to simulate capital deployment, arbitrage opportunities, and market behavior
  • Run scenario analyses to forecast outcomes under different assumptions
  • Evaluate risk and performance implications of potential actions
  • Provide insights to DAT, PIE, FLO, and other engines for planning and decision-making
  • Maintain historical simulation records for backtesting and validation
  • Support stress testing of system-wide processes and governance rules

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Execute actual trades or arbitrage opportunities (DAT)
  • Govern compliance or capital thresholds (GGP)
  • Generate canonical product intelligence (PIE)
  • Publish content or manage engagement (CDE)
  • Record financial ledgers (FLO)

SIM only simulates and analyzes; it does not perform operational actions.

5. Inputs (What This Engine Consumes)

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

SIM May Decide Autonomously:

  • How to model scenarios and parameters
  • Which scenarios to prioritize for analysis
  • Risk scoring and outcome evaluation

SIM Must Not Decide:

  • Actual capital deployment or execution timing
  • Governance enforcement
  • Product or content strategies

SIM is a forecasting and analysis engine, not an executor.

8. Governance & Constraints

  • All simulation and scenario data must comply with GGP rules
  • Models must be auditable and reproducible
  • Scenario assumptions and parameters must be clearly documented
  • Outputs must not bypass governance or execution layers

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Modeling inaccuracies
  • Incomplete scenario coverage
  • Data inconsistency or missing inputs

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Downstream engines make suboptimal decisions based on flawed scenarios
  • Misestimated risk or capital exposure

Blast Radius:

  • Primarily affects planning and decision-making outcomes; no direct operational or financial impact

Containment strategy:

  • Versioned simulation models
  • Validation and sensitivity analysis
  • Alerts for anomalous scenario results

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Accuracy, predictive power, and scenario coverage
  • Time Horizon: Strategic to near-term planning; real-time optional for stress testing

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Product and market identifiers (PIE)
  • Execution references (DAT)
  • Financial metrics and ledgers (FLO)
  • Governance flags and policies (GGP)
  • Signal datasets (SIG)

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Basic scenario simulation with historical data
  • Risk evaluation and outcome reports
  • Integration with DAT, PIE, FLO for scenario inputs and outputs
  • Historical archives for backtesting

MVP Excludes:

  • Real-time adaptive simulation with predictive AI
  • Full-scale multi-market optimization beyond MVP scope
  • Automated capital allocation or action execution

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Historical data-driven scenario modeling
  • Phase 2: Multi-variable and cross-engine simulations
  • Phase 3: Predictive, adaptive, and AI-assisted scenario planning
