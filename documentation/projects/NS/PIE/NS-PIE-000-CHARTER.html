NSDC Engine Charter — PIE (Product InsightIQ Engine)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Product InsightIQ Engine

Acronym: PIE

Engine Type: Intelligence / Knowledge

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to consolidate, analyze, and provide structured product and market intelligence to support informed decision-making across NSDC, enabling actionable insights for product development, sourcing, and market deployment.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Aggregate product and market data from internal and external sources
  • Normalize and structure product identity, attributes, and variants
  • Analyze trends, demand signals, and historical performance
  • Provide insights for product research, sourcing, and strategic decisions
  • Feed actionable intelligence to downstream engines (DAT, CDE)
  • Maintain canonical product truth and historical performance records

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Execute trades or arbitrage opportunities
  • Govern capital or risk thresholds
  • Publish content or manage attention campaigns
  • Maintain financial ledgers or accounting records
  • Directly enforce operational actions without downstream engine mediation

PIE is the intelligence provider, not the executor or controller.

5. Inputs (What This Engine Consumes)

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

PIE May Decide Autonomously:

  • How to normalize, structure, and store product data
  • How to prioritize trends and insights for relevance

PIE Must Not Decide:

  • When or where to execute trades
  • How capital is deployed
  • How content is distributed

PIE informs decisions but does not act on them directly.

8. Governance & Constraints

  • Must comply with GGP-defined policies on data usage and reporting
  • Ensure historical and canonical data integrity
  • Maintain versioning of insights and intelligence outputs
  • Audit-ready data pipelines and transformation processes

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Data feed interruptions
  • Normalization errors
  • Trend misinterpretation

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Downstream engines receive incorrect intelligence leading to suboptimal execution

Blast Radius:

  • Decision impact on DAT or CDE outcomes
  • No direct financial or compliance violation

Containment strategy:

  • Data validation layers
  • Automated integrity checks
  • Logging and alerts

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Accuracy, completeness, and actionable intelligence
  • Time Horizon: Continuous / strategic insight over daily and long-term horizons

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Product IDs and canonical references (IDN)
  • Market identifiers
  • Trend and signal schemas
  • Historical performance records
  • Governance flags and constraints (GGP)

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Aggregation and normalization of product data
  • Historical performance tracking
  • Market trend identification
  • Feeding structured insights to DAT and CDE

MVP Excludes:

  • Predictive ML for arbitrage
  • Full automation of sourcing or content strategies
  • External API integrations beyond MVP sources

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Structured aggregation and canonical product records
  • Phase 2: Advanced trend analysis and predictive intelligence
  • Phase 3: Fully integrated intelligence layer for real-time decisioning across NSDC
