NSDC Engine Charter — DAT (Digital Arbitrage Tooling)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Digital Arbitrage Tooling

Acronym: DAT

Engine Type: Execution / Arbitrage / Intelligence Application

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to identify arbitrage opportunities across multiple marketplaces, analyze profitability, and execute trades or actions in alignment with insights gathered from PIE and other NSDC engines.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Analyze multiple marketplaces for arbitrage opportunities
  • Integrate and validate insights from PIE (Product InsightIQ Engine)
  • Execute trades or purchase/sell operations across marketplaces
  • Track and report executed actions and outcomes
  • Log performance and maintain audit-ready execution history
  • Interface with MUX for marketplace connectivity and API handling

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Govern compliance or risk policies (GGP)
  • Maintain financial ledgers or accounting beyond execution tracking (FLO)
  • Generate product canonical data (PIE)
  • Publish content or manage engagement (CDE)

DAT is focused on arbitrage execution and monitoring only.

5. Inputs (What This Engine Consumes)

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

DAT May Decide Autonomously:

  • Which opportunities to execute based on predefined thresholds
  • Retry or cancel executions in response to errors or market changes
  • Logging and reporting formats

DAT Must Not Decide:

  • Governance policy enforcement (GGP)
  • Financial ledger maintenance beyond execution tracking (FLO)
  • Product canonicalization (PIE)
  • Content publication or engagement (CDE)

8. Governance & Constraints

  • Must comply with GGP-defined policies and limits
  • All executions must be logged for audit and reporting
  • Must use MUX interfaces for marketplace connectivity
  • Execution thresholds must be predefined and auditable

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Market data discrepancies
  • Execution errors due to connectivity or throttling
  • Miscalculation of arbitrage profit

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Losses due to failed execution or delayed action
  • Missed arbitrage opportunities impacting ROI

Blast Radius:

  • Primarily affects financial performance (FLO) and market operations
  • Governance and audit (GGP) may be impacted if logs are incomplete

Containment strategy:

  • Retry mechanisms and backoff strategies
  • Alerts for failures
  • Performance logging for post-incident review

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Maximizing arbitrage profit while respecting governance constraints
  • Time Horizon: Near real-time to real-time execution depending on market conditions

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Marketplace APIs and credentials (MUX)
  • Product and pricing data (PIE)
  • Execution signals (SIG)
  • Governance flags and thresholds (GGP)
  • Historical execution logs for analysis

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Identification of arbitrage opportunities
  • Execution of trades across at least 2 marketplaces
  • Logging and reporting of executed actions
  • Integration with PIE and MUX for data and connectivity

MVP Excludes:

  • Predictive execution beyond defined thresholds
  • Full marketplace coverage beyond MVP scope
  • Governance or financial ledger creation beyond logging

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Basic identification and execution on limited marketplaces
  • Phase 2: Expanded coverage, automated retries, performance logging
  • Phase 3: Predictive, AI-assisted arbitrage and optimization across multiple markets
