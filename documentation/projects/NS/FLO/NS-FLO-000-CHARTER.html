NSDC Engine Charter — FLO (Financial Ledger Orchestration)

1. Engine Identity

Engine Name: Financial Ledger Orchestration

Acronym: FLO

Engine Type: Ledger / Accounting / Execution Support

2. Mission Statement (One Sentence)

This engine exists to record, consolidate, and reconcile all financial transactions within the NSDC ecosystem, providing a canonical source of truth for capital flow, performance tracking, and audit compliance.

3. Core Responsibilities (Allowed Actions)

  • Maintain a unified, canonical ledger of all financial transactions
  • Reconcile capital movements between engines and marketplaces
  • Provide accurate reporting for P&L, balance sheets, and cash flows
  • Integrate with execution engines (DAT) for transaction validation
  • Feed financial summaries and insights to PIE, CDE, and administrative systems
  • Ensure audit readiness and maintain immutable transaction logs

4. Explicit Non-Responsibilities (Forbidden Actions)

  • Make capital allocation or trading decisions (DAT)
  • Generate product or market intelligence (PIE)
  • Publish content or manage engagement (CDE)
  • Define governance rules (GGP)
  • Directly manage external marketplace integrations (MUX)

FLO is strictly the ledger and reporting engine.

5. Inputs (What This Engine Consumes)

6. Outputs (What This Engine Produces)

7. Authority & Decision Rights

FLO May Decide Autonomously:

  • Validate transaction integrity
  • Reconcile ledger balances
  • Generate reports from ledger data

FLO Must Not Decide:

  • Capital allocation or execution strategy
  • Governance policy enforcement
  • Product or market opportunity selection

FLO is a canonical financial observer and recorder, not an executor.

8. Governance & Constraints

  • All ledger entries must comply with GGP-defined policies
  • Must maintain immutable, auditable records
  • Ensure reconciliations and validation procedures are enforced
  • Integration with external APIs must comply with security and compliance standards

9. Failure Modes & Blast Radius

Expected Failures:

  • Delayed reconciliation
  • Minor ledger inconsistencies due to timing

Worst-Case Failure:

  • Inaccurate financial reporting
  • Compromised audit readiness

Blast Radius:

  • Impacts all engines relying on canonical financial data
  • No direct market execution impact if DAT operates independently, but may misreport P&L

Containment strategy:

  • Reconciliation alerts
  • Manual overrides with logging
  • Immutable historical snapshots

10. Time Horizon & Optimization Target

  • Optimizes for: Accuracy, completeness, and auditability
  • Time Horizon: Continuous recording with periodic reconciliations

11. Interfaces & Shared Primitives

  • Canonical entity IDs (IDN)
  • Product and transaction IDs (PIE / DAT)
  • Governance flags and approvals (GGP)
  • Order and execution objects (DAT)
  • Ledger and audit schemas

12. MVP Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

MVP Includes:

  • Transaction logging and ledger maintenance
  • Integration with DAT for executed trades
  • P&L and cashflow reporting
  • Audit log creation

MVP Excludes:

  • Predictive finance modeling
  • Integration with external accounting systems beyond MVP
  • Automated capital allocation or trading

13. Evolution Path (Conceptual Only)

  • Phase 1: Canonical ledger + basic reporting
  • Phase 2: Real-time reconciliation and dashboards
  • Phase 3: Predictive analytics and integrated financial modeling
