1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the compliance framework governing legal, tax, regulatory, data, and operational obligations across all Northfield Solidarity entities.

This SOP exists to:

  • Ensure all entities operate in compliance with applicable laws and regulations
  • Coordinate compliance obligations across finance, operations, IP, and brand
  • Reduce legal, tax, and regulatory risk
  • Provide a unified escalation and remediation structure

Risks mitigated:

  • Regulatory penalties and fines
  • Tax noncompliance or misclassification
  • Data privacy violations
  • Contractual or IP misuse exposure
  • Veil-piercing and governance failures

Outcome ensured:

  • Consistent, documented compliance posture
  • Early detection and remediation of compliance issues
  • Audit-ready governance framework

2. Scope

Entities Covered:

  • Northfield Solidarity (Parent / HoldCo)
  • All operating, holding, IP, and brand entities

Roles Covered:

  • Compliance Officer / Governance Lead
  • Legal Counsel (internal or external)
  • Finance Lead / Controller
  • Operations Managers
  • Brand and IP Managers
  • Managing Member / Board

Activities Included:

  • Legal and regulatory compliance
  • Tax compliance coordination
  • Data privacy and security oversight
  • Contract and IP compliance
  • Internal policy adherence
  • Compliance monitoring and reporting

Explicit Exclusions:

  • Day-to-day operational execution (covered by respective SOPs)
  • Accounting mechanics (Finance SOP)

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Compliance is mandatory across all entities and roles
  • Accountability is assigned and documented
  • Issues are escalated early, not concealed
  • Documentation is required for all compliance activities
  • Substance-over-form governs compliance interpretation
  • Compliance supports, not blocks, scalable operations

6. Process Overview

Inputs:

  • Applicable laws and regulations
  • Internal SOPs and policies
  • Compliance reports and audits
  • Incident reports

Key Steps:

  1. Compliance requirement identification
  2. Policy and SOP alignment
  3. Monitoring and controls
  4. Incident detection and reporting
  5. Remediation and corrective action
  6. Review and continuous improvement

Outputs:

  • Compliance reports
  • Remediation actions
  • Audit findings and resolutions
  • Updated policies and SOPs

7. Detailed Procedures

7.1 Compliance Identification

  • Action: Identify applicable legal, tax, regulatory, and data obligations per entity
  • Owner: Compliance Officer / Legal Counsel
  • Output: Compliance obligation register

7.2 Policy & SOP Alignment

  • Action: Ensure SOPs and policies align with compliance requirements
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Approved compliance-mapped SOPs

7.3 Monitoring & Controls

  • Action: Monitor adherence through reviews, checklists, and system controls
  • Owner: Compliance Officer / Operations Managers
  • Output: Compliance monitoring reports

7.4 Incident Reporting

  • Action: Report suspected noncompliance promptly
  • Owner: Any staff → Compliance Officer
  • Output: Incident report log

7.5 Remediation & Corrective Action

  • Action: Investigate incidents, implement corrective actions, document outcomes
  • Owner: Compliance Officer / Legal / Finance (as applicable)
  • Output: Remediation plan and resolution record

7.6 Reporting & Review

  • Action: Prepare periodic compliance reports for management
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Compliance status reports

8. Controls & Compliance

  • Mandatory compliance training for relevant roles
  • Periodic internal compliance reviews
  • External advisors engaged as needed
  • Documented approvals and exceptions
  • Separation of duties enforced

9. Records & Documentation

10. KPIs & Monitoring

  • Number of compliance incidents
  • Time to incident resolution
  • Audit findings severity
  • Training completion rates
  • Repeat issues identified

11. Exceptions & Escalation

  • Material compliance breaches escalated immediately to Managing Member / Board
  • Regulatory inquiries handled by Legal Counsel
  • Emergency actions documented post-facto

12. Tools & Systems

  • Compliance management tools
  • Document management system
  • Accounting and reporting systems
  • Security and access control systems
  • Proprietary compliance dashboard (future)

13. Security & Access Control

  • View access: Compliance, Legal, Management
  • Edit access: Compliance Officer
  • Approval authority: Managing Member / Board
  • Audit logs required for all changes

14. Audit & Review

  • Annual compliance review
  • Triggered reviews upon regulatory change or incident
  • Responsible Party: Compliance Officer / Governance Lead
