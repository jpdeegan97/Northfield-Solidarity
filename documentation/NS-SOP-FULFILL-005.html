1. Purpose

This SOP governs the execution, oversight, and control of order fulfillment, logistics, inventory handling, and third-party fulfillment relationships across the Northfield Solidarity ecosystem.

This SOP exists to:

  • Ensure reliable, cost-controlled delivery of goods and services
  • Maintain brand consistency and customer satisfaction
  • Prevent unauthorized spend, inventory leakage, or operational risk
  • Align fulfillment execution with Finance, Revenue, Compliance, and Parent governance

Risks mitigated:

  • Cost overruns and margin erosion
  • Inventory loss, shrinkage, or misallocation
  • Vendor non-performance or contract drift
  • Compliance failures related to shipping, customs, or consumer protection

Outcome ensured:

  • Predictable, auditable fulfillment execution
  • Clear accountability across logistics flows
  • Compliance-aligned escalation and exception handling

2. Scope

Entities Covered:

  • Northfield Solidarity (Parent / HoldCo)
  • All operating entities performing fulfillment or logistics functions
  • Approved third-party logistics (3PL) providers

Roles Covered:

  • Operations Lead
  • Fulfillment Manager
  • Inventory Manager
  • Finance Lead
  • Compliance Officer
  • Authorized Signatories

Activities Included:

  • Order intake and release to fulfillment
  • Inventory storage, picking, packing, and shipping
  • Vendor and 3PL management
  • Returns and reverse logistics
  • Fulfillment cost tracking and reconciliation
  • Exception handling and escalation

Explicit Exclusions:

  • Revenue recognition (Revenue Flow SOP v1.2)
  • Capital expenditures and asset acquisition (Capital Flow SOP v1.2)
  • Cash disbursements and reimbursements (Finance / Cash Flow SOP v1.2)

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Fulfillment execution must align with approved cost and service thresholds
  • No fulfillment activity may create unapproved capital or contractual obligations
  • Inventory is tracked, reconciled, and auditable at all times
  • Third-party vendors operate only under approved agreements
  • Exceptions trigger Compliance SOP v1.2
  • Parent / HoldCo retains override authority for material deviations

5. Roles & Responsibilities

6. Process Overview

Inputs:

  • Approved customer orders
  • Inventory availability data
  • Vendor / 3PL agreements

Key Steps:

  1. Order release to fulfillment
  2. Inventory allocation and pick/pack
  3. Shipment execution
  4. Delivery confirmation
  5. Returns handling (if applicable)
  6. Cost reconciliation
  7. Compliance review and escalation
  8. Documentation storage

Outputs:

  • Fulfilled orders
  • Updated inventory records
  • Fulfillment cost reports
  • Compliance Event Logs (if applicable)

7. Detailed Procedures

7.1 Order Release

  • Action: Validate order approval and release to fulfillment system
  • Owner: Fulfillment Manager
  • Output: Authorized fulfillment request

7.2 Inventory Allocation

  • Action: Reserve inventory; confirm availability
  • Owner: Inventory Manager
  • Output: Inventory allocation record

7.3 Pick, Pack, and Ship

  • Action: Execute fulfillment via internal team or approved 3PL
  • Owner: Fulfillment Manager
  • Output: Shipment tracking and confirmation

7.4 Delivery Confirmation

  • Action: Confirm delivery status and customer receipt
  • Owner: Fulfillment Manager
  • Output: Closed fulfillment order

7.5 Returns & Reverse Logistics

  • Action: Process returns according to approved return policy
  • Owner: Fulfillment Manager
  • Output: Updated inventory and refund triggers
  • Compliance: Exceptions logged if policy deviations occur

7.6 Cost Reconciliation

  • Action: Reconcile shipping, storage, and handling costs
  • Owner: Finance Lead
  • Output: Reconciled fulfillment cost report

7.7 Compliance Escalation

  • Action: Escalate cost overruns, shrinkage, vendor failures, or regulatory issues
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Compliance Event Log and Parent notification if thresholds exceeded

7.8 Documentation Storage

  • Action: Store shipping records, inventory logs, vendor invoices, and approvals
  • Owner: Operations Lead
  • Output: Audit-ready records

8. Controls & Compliance

  • Inventory counts reconciled monthly
  • Approved vendor and 3PL list maintained
  • Cost thresholds enforced with automatic escalation
  • Segregation of duties between operations and finance
  • Compliance SOP v1.2 governs all exceptions

9. Records & Documentation

10. KPIs & Monitoring

  • Order fulfillment time
  • Delivery success rate
  • Fulfillment cost per order
  • Inventory accuracy rate
  • Shrinkage percentage
  • Compliance exception frequency

11. Exceptions & Escalation

  • Cost overruns: Fulfillment → Finance → Compliance → Parent if material
  • Inventory shrinkage above threshold: Immediate Compliance escalation
  • Vendor non-performance: Operations Lead → Compliance → Parent
  • Emergency fulfillment actions logged post-event

  • Approval authority: Authorized Signatories / Parent

14. Audit & Review

  • Quarterly vendor performance assessment
