1. Purpose

This SOP governs the classification, approval, movement, and monitoring of capital across the Northfield Solidarity entity structure. It establishes capital as a distinct economic flow, separate from revenue, intercompany transactions, and operating cash.

This SOP exists to:

  • Prevent misclassification of capital as revenue or loans
  • Ensure Parent-controlled capitalization strategy
  • Provide audit- and tax-defensible capital documentation
  • Integrate capital activity into the Compliance enforcement framework

Risks mitigated:

  • IRS recharacterization of funds
  • Phantom income or improper equity dilution
  • Veil-piercing through undocumented capital movement
  • Inconsistent capitalization practices

Outcome ensured:

  • Clear, intentional, and auditable capital flows

2. Scope

Entities Covered:

  • Northfield Solidarity (Parent / HoldCo)
  • All wholly-owned operating, holding, IP, and special-purpose entities

Roles Covered:

  • Managing Member / Board
  • Finance Lead / Controller
  • Compliance Officer
  • Authorized Signatories

Activities Included:

  • Capital contributions
  • Capital withdrawals and distributions
  • Equity injections
  • Capital support outside normal operations

Explicit Exclusions:

  • Revenue receipts (Revenue Flow SOP)
  • Intercompany loans (Intercompany Transactions SOP)
  • Expense reimbursements (Finance / Cash Flow SOP)

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Capital is not revenue, income, or repayment
  • All capital originates from Parent authority
  • Every capital movement requires documented intent
  • Economic substance governs over form
  • Capital events trigger compliance review
  • Capital activity must preserve corporate separateness

5. Roles & Responsibilities

6. Capital Classification Framework

All inbound or outbound funds must be classified as one of the following before execution:

  1. Capital Contribution
  2. Capital Distribution
  3. Equity Injection

Funds that do not meet capital criteria must be reclassified under the appropriate SOP.

7. Capital Intent Declaration (CID)

Every capital event must be accompanied by a Capital Intent Declaration, including:

  • Source entity
  • Recipient entity
  • Purpose (growth, stabilization, acquisition, etc.)
  • Expected duration
  • Ownership impact
  • Relationship to strategic plan

CID approval is mandatory prior to execution.

8. Process Overview

Inputs:

  • Capital request or strategic plan
  • Capital Intent Declaration

Key Steps:

  1. Capital need identification
  2. Classification and CID preparation
  3. Parent approval
  4. Compliance review
  5. Execution of capital movement
  6. Recording and reconciliation
  7. Monitoring and review

Outputs:

  • Executed capital transaction
  • Updated equity accounts
  • Compliance Event Log entry

9. Detailed Procedures

9.1 Capital Request Initiation

  • Action: Submit capital request and draft CID
  • Owner: Subsidiary Manager / Finance
  • Output: Capital request packet

9.2 Classification & Validation

  • Action: Validate capital classification and accounting treatment
  • Owner: Finance Lead
  • Output: Approved classification

9.3 Parent Approval

  • Action: Review strategic alignment and approve or reject
  • Owner: Managing Member / Board
  • Output: Signed capital approval

9.4 Compliance Trigger & Review

  • Action: Log capital event and assess severity
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Compliance Event Log entry

9.5 Execution

  • Action: Transfer funds or record equity action
  • Owner: Authorized Signatories
  • Output: Completed capital movement

9.6 Recording & Reconciliation

  • Action: Record in equity accounts and reconcile
  • Owner: Finance Lead
  • Output: Updated ledgers

9.7 Monitoring & Review

  • Action: Review CID adherence and sunset conditions
  • Owner: Compliance Officer / Parent
  • Output: Ongoing capital status

10. Controls & Compliance Safeguards

  • Mandatory Parent approval for all capital events
  • CID required before execution
  • Compliance-triggered logging and review
  • Quarterly equity reconciliation
  • Annual capital strategy review

11. Records & Documentation

12. KPIs & Monitoring

  • Number of unclassified capital movements (target: zero)
  • Time from request to approval
  • Capital deployed vs strategic plan
  • Compliance findings related to capital

13. Exceptions & Escalation

  • No capital execution without Parent approval
  • Emergency capital actions require ratification within 10 business days
  • Repeated capital exceptions trigger governance review

14. Tools & Systems

  • Accounting and ERP systems
  • Compliance Event Log
  • Document management system
  • Proprietary capital management platform (future)

15. Security & Access Control

  • View access: Parent, Finance, Compliance
  • Edit access: Finance Lead
  • Approval authority: Managing Member / Board

16. Audit & Review

  • Quarterly internal capital review
  • Annual independent audit review
  • Updates triggered by ownership or regulatory changes
