1. Purpose

This SOP defines the authority, responsibilities, and decision-making framework of the Northfield Solidarity Parent / HoldCo entity. It serves as the constitutional layer governing all subsidiaries, intercompany relationships, capital decisions, and escalations.

This SOP exists to:

  • Codify Parent authority across all entities
  • Define escalation, override, and arbitration rules
  • Protect corporate separateness while enabling centralized governance
  • Provide a defensible governance and veil-preservation framework

Risks mitigated:

  • Governance ambiguity
  • Unauthorized subsidiary actions
  • Veil-piercing exposure
  • Inconsistent intercompany decision-making

Outcome ensured:

  • Clear, enforceable Parent control with documented authority

2. Scope

Entities Covered:

  • Northfield Solidarity (Parent / HoldCo)
  • All wholly-owned subsidiaries, OpCos, IP entities, and SPVs

Roles Covered:

  • Managing Member
  • Board of Managers (if applicable)
  • Compliance Officer
  • Finance Lead / Controller
  • Authorized Signatories

Activities Included:

  • Strategic approvals and overrides
  • Capital allocation decisions
  • Intercompany arbitration
  • IP ownership and licensing authority
  • Compliance escalations

Explicit Exclusions:

  • Day-to-day operational execution
  • Routine transactional processing

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Parent authority is strategic and supervisory, not operational
  • Subsidiaries operate independently within approved SOPs
  • Reserved powers cannot be delegated
  • All overrides must be documented and time-bound
  • Economic substance governs governance decisions
  • Compliance enforcement is mandatory

5. Roles & Responsibilities

6. Parent Authority Framework

6.1 Reserved Powers (Non-Delegable)

The following decisions are exclusively reserved to the Parent:

  • Capital contributions and distributions
  • Intercompany policy approval and exceptions
  • IP ownership transfers or licensing overrides
  • Approval of pricing below economic floor
  • Formation or dissolution of subsidiaries
  • Approval of extraordinary transactions

6.2 Authority Matrix

7. Escalation & Override Process

Inputs:

  • Triggering event or request
  • Supporting analysis and documentation

Key Steps:

  1. Escalation triggered by SOP or Compliance
  2. Parent review and analysis
  3. Approval, rejection, or conditional override
  4. Documentation and logging
  5. Time-bound monitoring

Outputs:

  • Documented Parent decision
  • Compliance Event Log entry

8. Detailed Procedures

8.1 Escalation Initiation

  • Action: Submit escalation request with supporting memo
  • Owner: Subsidiary Manager / Compliance Officer
  • Output: Escalation packet

8.2 Parent Review

  • Action: Analyze economic, legal, and strategic impact
  • Owner: Managing Member / Board
  • Output: Decision rationale

8.3 Approval or Override

  • Action: Approve, reject, or approve with conditions
  • Owner: Parent Authority
  • Output: Signed Parent decision

8.4 Documentation & Logging

  • Action: Log decision in Compliance Event Log
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Audit-ready record

8.5 Monitoring & Sunset

  • Action: Monitor compliance with conditions and expiration
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Closure or renewal decision

9. Controls & Safeguards

  • Mandatory Compliance involvement for all escalations
  • Dual-signature requirement for critical decisions
  • Time-bound overrides with expiration dates
  • Quarterly review of Parent decisions

10. Records & Documentation

11. KPIs & Monitoring

  • Number of escalations by category
  • Override frequency and duration
  • Repeat escalations from same entity
  • Compliance findings related to governance

12. Exceptions

  • No exceptions to Reserved Powers
  • Emergency actions require post-event ratification within 10 business days

13. Tools & Systems

14. Security & Access Control

  • View access: Parent, Compliance, Finance
  • Edit access: Compliance Officer
  • Approval authority: Managing Member / Board

15. Audit & Review
