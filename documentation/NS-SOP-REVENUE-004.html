1. Purpose

This SOP governs the recognition, collection, recording, and allocation of revenue across the Northfield Solidarity entity ecosystem, ensuring clear segregation from capital and loan flows, and compliance with accounting, tax, and governance regulations.

This SOP exists to:

  • Ensure revenue is recognized in accordance with GAAP/IFRS and tax standards
  • Maintain accurate, auditable revenue streams
  • Prevent commingling with capital, loans, or other financial flows
  • Enable timely reporting, reconciliation, and analysis of revenue performance

Risks mitigated:

  • Misstated revenue or financial reporting errors
  • Revenue leakage or delayed recognition
  • IRS or tax compliance issues
  • Improper intercompany revenue treatment

Outcome ensured:

  • Accurate, timely, and auditable revenue accounting
  • Proper segregation from capital and other financial flows
  • Defensible intercompany revenue allocations
  • Integrated escalation and compliance alignment

2. Scope

Entities Covered:

  • Northfield Solidarity (Parent / HoldCo)
  • All revenue-generating subsidiary LLCs and operating entities

Roles Covered:

  • Finance Lead / Controller
  • Revenue Managers
  • Accounts Receivable (AR) Team
  • Compliance Officer
  • Authorized Signatories

Activities Included:

  • Contract review and approval
  • Revenue classification and memo creation
  • Invoicing and billing
  • Cash collection
  • Revenue recognition in ledger
  • Intercompany revenue allocations
  • Reporting and reconciliation
  • Compliance escalations

Explicit Exclusions:

  • Capital contributions (Capital Flow SOP v1.2)
  • Intercompany loans (Intercompany Transactions SOP v1.0)
  • Expense reimbursements (Finance / Cash Flow SOP v1.2)

3. Definitions

4. Governing Principles

  • Revenue must be recognized according to GAAP/IFRS and local tax rules
  • Revenue is never commingled with capital or debt flows (Capital Flow SOP v1.2)
  • Every revenue transaction must have supporting documentation (contract, PO, invoice)
  • Intercompany revenue must be arm’s-length and documented with dual approval (Intercompany SOP v1.0)
  • All steps must be traceable, auditable, and linked to Compliance SOP v1.2
  • Exceptions require defined thresholds, Parent / HoldCo review, and written approval

5. Roles & Responsibilities

6. Process Overview

Inputs:

  • Signed contracts, POs, or sales agreements
  • Customer payment details
  • Issued invoices
  • Escalation requests

Key Steps:

  1. Contract review and approval
  2. Revenue classification and memo creation
  3. Invoice issuance
  4. Payment collection
  5. Revenue recognition in ledger
  6. Intercompany allocation (if applicable)
  7. Reconciliation and reporting
  8. Compliance review and escalation
  9. Secure documentation storage

Outputs:

  • Recognized revenue entries
  • Updated AR and ledger accounts
  • Audit-ready documentation and memos
  • Compliance Event Log entries for exceptions

7. Detailed Procedures

7.1 Contract Review

  • Action: Verify contract terms, pricing, delivery obligations, and revenue recognition triggers
  • Owner: Revenue Manager
  • Output: Approved contract for invoicing with classification notes

7.2 Revenue Classification & Memo

  • Action: Determine recognition timing, intercompany allocation, and document in Revenue Classification Memo
  • Owner: Finance Lead with Revenue Manager review
  • Output: Classification Memo attached to contract and ledger entry

7.3 Invoice Issuance

  • Action: Generate and send invoice according to terms; attach contract and classification memo
  • Owner: AR Team
  • Output: Issued and tracked invoice

7.4 Payment Collection

  • Action: Collect payments via approved methods, confirm receipt, attach to AR record
  • Owner: AR Team
  • Output: Payment confirmation in accounting system

7.5 Revenue Recognition

  • Action: Record revenue in ledger according to classification and recognition rules
  • Owner: Finance Lead / Controller
  • Output: Recognized revenue entries, linked to invoices and classification memos

7.6 Intercompany Allocation

  • Action: Allocate revenue at arm’s-length rates; attach signed intercompany revenue agreement if applicable
  • Owner: Authorized Signatory and Finance Lead
  • Output: Intercompany revenue ledger entries with supporting documentation
  • Control: Dual approval required for all intercompany revenue allocations
  • Compliance: Compliance Officer verifies accuracy and triggers escalation if thresholds exceeded

7.7 Reconciliation & Reporting

  • Action: Reconcile AR, ledger, and bank deposits monthly; prepare reports
  • Owner: Finance Lead / Controller
  • Output: Reconciled revenue reports and audit-ready documentation
  • Compliance: Exception triggers logged

7.8 Compliance Escalation

  • Action: Any exception, unusual transaction, or threshold breach triggers Compliance SOP v1.2
  • Owner: Compliance Officer
  • Output: Escalation to Parent / HoldCo if required, logged in Compliance Event Log

7.9 Documentation Storage

  • Action: Store all contracts, classification memos, invoices, approvals, and confirmations in centralized secure repository
  • Owner: Finance Lead
  • Output: Complete, auditable records

8. Controls & Compliance

  • Dual approval for intercompany revenue allocations
  • Segregation of duties: contract review, billing, collections, ledger entry
  • Defined thresholds for exceptions and write-offs
  • Mandatory Compliance SOP v1.2 logging
  • Quarterly review by Finance Lead / Controller
  • Annual internal and external audit readiness

9. Records & Documentation

10. KPIs & Monitoring

  • Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
  • Timeliness of invoicing
  • Revenue recognition accuracy
  • AR aging and collection efficiency
  • Accuracy of intercompany revenue allocations
  • Compliance escalation response time
  • Audit readiness compliance

11. Exceptions & Escalation

  • Late payments: Revenue Manager → Finance Lead → Compliance Officer → Parent / HoldCo if unresolved
  • Billing errors: corrected with memo and approval
  • Intercompany allocation exceptions require Parent / HoldCo approval
  • Emergency adjustments logged in Compliance Event Log

  • Accounting Software (QuickBooks, NetSuite, or equivalent)

13. Security & Access Control

  • View access: Finance Team, Revenue Managers, Compliance Officer, Managing Member
  • Edit access: AR Team, Finance Lead
  • Approval authority: Managing Member / Authorized Signatories
  • Audit logs maintained for all approvals and transactions

14. Audit & Review

  • Monthly reconciliation and review by Finance Lead
  • Quarterly intercompany revenue review
  • Annual internal and external audit readiness
  • Responsible Party: Finance Lead / Controller
  • Compliance SOP ensures escalations are tracked and addressed
